# McKay Electrical Services — Standard Operating Procedures

**Version:** 3.1 (Draft for Review)
**Effective Date:** 1 March 2024
**Owner:** Operations Manager

---

## 1. AFTER HOURS CALLS

In providing essential service support to businesses, after-hours callouts are a
condition of employment requiring mutual co-operation.

When after-hours calls require an employee to work between the finish of work on
one day and the start of work the following day, the employee is entitled to delay
normal starting the following morning by the time necessary to ensure a
**six-hour break** has occurred between the finish of work and the start of work
of the respective days.

If the after-hours call is between midnight and 6:00 am, the employee may delay
starting the following morning by the equivalent time spent in travel and on the
job the previous night. Normal rates shall be paid for delay time in addition to
the callout payment.

If the employee is required to start at 7:30 am and has not had a six-hour break
since the last working day, the period between 7:30 am and the time at which the
employee would have started after the break shall be paid at overtime.

All changes in address or telephone number must be immediately reported to the
**Branch Supervisor** for updating personnel and callout files.

---

## 2. TIMESHEETS

The timesheet (either paper or electronic) is the only official document used for
recording service time and calculation of wages.

**Daily Timesheets** are to be completed at the time work is completed and handed
to the applicable manager by **10:00 am** the following day.

**Weekly Timesheets.** Each day's hours are to be completed daily and the
completed weekly timesheet handed to the applicable manager by **10:00 am** the
following Monday.

All hard copy timesheets must be clearly written and easily readable, totalled and
signed by each employee.

---

## 3. LEAVE ENTITLEMENT

Holiday entitlement is as per the employment agreement. These holidays are taken
in accordance with the Holidays Act and at a time mutually agreed. All leave is
preferably to be taken in the year it is due. Employees schedule their annual
leave arrangements after consultation with their manager. Every effort is made to
ensure generally that staff are able to take holidays at dates of their preference
within the above requirements.

---

## 4. NOTIFICATION OF ABSENCE FROM WORK

If you require release from work during the day for unavoidable reasons such as
professional appointments, this should be arranged in consultation with your
supervisor. Paid time off will be granted at the discretion of the company and
subject to circumstances.

If you are unavoidably detained from work you should inform your supervisor as
soon as possible, stating the anticipated length of your absence. Continued
absence without notification for a period exceeding **five days** can be grounds
for termination of employment.

---

## 5. GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

The procedure for resolving personal grievances is contained in the Employment
Agreement.

## 6. SAFETY

We recognise the necessity to promote safe working conditions and work habits of
all employees. This requires the full co-operation of everyone. It is your
responsibility to report any unsafe conditions to your supervisor immediately they
are noticed.

1. Always look out for hazards and remember to remove them, guard against them
   and warn others about them.
2. Smoking, including the use of electronic cigarettes, is not permitted in any
   company workplace in accordance with the Smoke-free Environments Act 2003.
3. Know where the fire extinguishers are in our company and how to use them if
   necessary.
4. Pay attention to safety notices and exit routes from our company and customers'
   premises.
5. Protective clothing or equipment provided must be worn where necessary.
6. Persons entrusted with on-job supervision of young people have particular
   responsibility for training in safe working habits.
7. All employees shall be aware of the contents of the Company Health and Safety
   Manual.
8. All employees shall comply with the safety requirements on customer's
   sites/premises.

## 7. COMPANY/CUSTOMER RELATIONSHIPS

As in all service industries, good relationships with clients at all levels of
contact are critical. Where possible and practical, good relationships are
fostered by assigning trade and technical staff to specific clients.

---

## 8. PERSONAL PROPERTY

No responsibility will be assumed by the company for the loss or damage to
personal property, valuables, etc., brought onto company or job site premises,
unless they are work-related items stored and locked in approved areas.

---

## 9. SERVICE EQUIPMENT ON ISSUE FROM THE COMPANY

Service equipment and plant required to be carried in service vans or issued from
store is a personal loan issue which is the individual's responsibility and must
be accounted for at periodic inventory checks. Cost for plant not accounted for
due to employee's negligence will be processed according to the Employment
Agreement.

---

## 10. WORK INJURIES AND DAMAGE TO COMPANY PROPERTY

All work injuries and/or damage to company property must be reported to the
supervisor or manager immediately, and an incident report form must be completed
immediately and sent to Head Office Reception. A First-Aid cabinet is kept in the
Branch Reception Offices and is for minor injuries only. All other injuries should
be handled by competent medical people. Medical certificates must be handed to
your supervisor on return to work when a medical clearance is necessary.

> **NOTE:** Supervisors are no longer required to accompany injured personnel to
> the doctor or hospital. Employees should arrange their own transport for medical
> appointments.

---

## 11. CLOTHING

At all times a reasonable standard of dress is required. Where the employer
provides a uniform, it is expected that the uniform be maintained in good
condition and worn well.

## 12. COMPANY VEHICLES

Refer to separate company vehicle policy.

## 13. GOOD HOUSEKEEPING

Good housekeeping reduces accidents and safeguards health. We encourage all staff
to maintain high standards of housekeeping.

---

## 14. WASTE AND SCRAP

Separate scrap from reclaimable waste. Know the location of and use designated
waste and scrap receptacles in your workshop.

Any copper wire surplus to a job is the property of the company and must be
recovered and returned. Any waste or scrap material drawn from a customer's job
is the property of that customer and is only to be removed at the customer's
specific request.

---

## 15. PRIVATE TELEPHONE CALLS

Although receiving very occasional private calls within work time is agreed to,
you are expected to make only urgent or necessary calls during tea or lunch
breaks. The company reserves the right to charge the employee for excessive
private use costs.

---

## 16. TERMINATIONS

Conditions relating to termination of employment are covered in the employment
agreement. The management at all times has the right to summarily discharge any
employee for proper cause. Proper cause includes, but is not limited to:

a.) Absence from work for two consecutive days without reasonable cause and
without notification to the employer.
b.) Neglect of duty of a recurring or serious nature.
c.) Continued failure to perform duties in a satisfactory and proper manner.
d.) Unauthorised possession and/or movement of company, client, or other workers
property.
e.) Falsification or being party to falsification of any company or client document
or record.
f.) Failure to follow cash/till handling procedures, and/or negligence or
carelessness when handling cash/cheques etc.
g.) Unauthorised use of company/client equipment or vehicles.
h.) Irresponsible use of fire protection or safety equipment.
i.) Wilful, deliberate or negligent acts which may cause injury or damage or
adversely affect quality or productivity.
j.) Wilful damage to property/equipment/product.
k.) Refusal to obey a lawful and reasonable instruction.
l.) Failure to follow safety requirements.
m.) Sexual harassment of another employee, customer or client.
n.) Intimidation of another employee, customer or client.
o.) Fighting and/or verbal abuse of another employee, customer, or client.

> **REMOVED from this SOP revision:** clauses (p) Consuming alcohol on company
> or client property without management's consent, (q) Driving under influence of
> alcohol, and (r) Possession of illicit drugs — these are now covered under the
> separate Drugs and Alcohol Policy document.

summarily dismissed. Theft, whether petty or major, is not tolerated.

## 21. CONFIDENTIALITY

All information concerning the employer or customers affairs gained by the
employee during the course of their employment shall be confidential and not
passed onto any third party.

---

## 22. WORKING ON OWN ACCOUNT

The employee shall not engage in any electrical or service work on his/her own
account except as set out below:

a.) For members of the employee's immediate family (spouse, parent, parent-in-law,
child, brother, sister, grandparent or grandchild).
b.) And when specifically authorised by the **Branch Manager** when the work has
a commercial value of more than **$1,500 including GST**.

---

## 24. DRUGS AND ALCOHOL

All employees shall adhere to the following standards of behaviour.

a.) Employees should be fit and ready to carry out their duties at all times.
b.) Consumption of alcohol during working hours is not permitted under any
circumstances unless prior approval is obtained from the **Operations Manager**.
c.) Employees under the influence of alcohol shall not be permitted to commence or
continue work at the workplace.
d.) Deliberate misuse of illegal drugs is prohibited as is the possession, use,
sale, or distribution of illegal drugs.
e.) Employees shall not be under the influence of illegal drugs during working
hours or while travelling to or from work in company vehicles.
f.) Employees shall advise their supervisor if they are taking prescribed drugs so
that appropriate occupational health and safety advice can be provided.

All employees shall comply with clients' policies and procedures with regard to
individual client sites.

---

## 25. SMOKING POLICY

There is to be no smoking (including electronic cigarettes) in any McKay facility
including garages, sheds, containers, offices and McKay controlled work sites.
All McKay vehicles are to be 100% smoke free.

Employees may smoke during break times only in allowed areas or public spaces.

---

## 27. EXTENDED WORK HOURS

At times there are valid reasons to work extended hours. However, the safety of
individuals and equipment must be assured.

The maximum work hours shall be as follows:

a.) For projects four weeks and under in length, **fourteen-hour days** may be
worked with a maximum consecutive number of days worked of **thirteen** before
a day off.
b.) For projects over four weeks in length the normal hours of work may be up to
**sixty hours per week**, with at least one day off per week. For critical
progress reasons this can be increased to **eighty-four hours per week** for a
maximum of two weeks, however no more than thirteen consecutive days.

The pay staff have been instructed not to pay for hours beyond the above
restrictions.

---

## 28. ACCOUNTS PROCEDURES

If the employee is engaging services from McKay, or purchasing equipment, for
their own private use, the employee shall remain completely independent from the
jobbing and accounting processes. All spare parts are to be ordered over the
spare parts counter.

---

## 29. ENTERING CUSTOMERS UNATTENDED PREMISES

The employee must not enter any unattended premises without the owner's express
permission. Contact should first be made with the property owner and their
permission sought prior to entry.
